Sacred Monasteries of Sikkim

Discover the spiritual heart of the Himalayas through these ancient centers of Buddhist learning

Most Sacred

Rumtek Monastery

Seat of Karma Kagyu Lineage

The most significant monastery in Sikkim, serving as the main seat of the Karma Kagyu lineage. Built in the 1960s, it houses precious religious artifacts and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding hills.

Founded: 1966 300+ Monks 5,800 ft

Pemayangtse Monastery

Perfect Sublime Lotus

One of the oldest and most important Nyingma monasteries in Sikkim, known for its stunning architecture and spiritual significance.

Founded: 1705 7,000 ft

Enchey Monastery

Solitary Temple

A 200-year-old monastery built on a site blessed by Lama Drupthob Karpo, offering panoramic views of Gangtok valley.

Founded: 1840 6,200 ft

Tashiding Monastery

Most Sacred in Sikkim

Considered the holiest monastery in Sikkim, situated on a heart-shaped hill between two sacred rivers.

Founded: 1717 4,200 ft

Sacred Prayer Flags

The colorful prayer flags you see throughout Sikkim carry mantras and prayers that are believed to be carried by the wind to bring peace, wisdom, and good fortune to all beings.

Blue - Sky & Space
White - Air & Wind
Red - Fire
Green - Water
Yellow - Earth

How to Reach Sikkim

By Air

Bagdogra Airport (IXB): 124 km from Gangtok

Pakyong Airport (PYG): 33 km from Gangtok

Regular flights from Delhi, Kolkata, Mumbai

By Rail/Road

New Jalpaiguri (NJP): 148 km from Gangtok

Siliguri: 114 km from Gangtok

Well connected by road with regular bus services

Permits Required

Nathula Pass: Special permit needed

Gurudongmar Lake: Protected area permit

Foreign tourists need special permits

Best Time to Visit

March - June: Pleasant weather

September - November: Clear mountain views

Avoid monsoons (July-August)
